Gel Simulations

Research Experience

In the Spring of 2016 I was selected to participate in a Summer NSF REU at Clemson University in order to work with Dr. Kuksenok on the study of hydrogel swelling induced by illumination or heating using computer simulations. By the end of the summer, we expanded that to include magnetic heating effects as well. I explored the hysteresis in the swelling and deswelling of illuminated and un-illuminated gels and worked to pursue a self-oscillating gel system. Much of my work, though, included behind the scenes improvements to the model and associated tools. I re-wrote sections of the C code for running simulations and added provisions for convective cooling and heating by ferromagnetic induction. I communicated my work in several presentations within Dr. Kuksenok's group and within the REU cohort. The end of the summer culminated with a poster presentation session in tandem with other Clemson summer research programs.
